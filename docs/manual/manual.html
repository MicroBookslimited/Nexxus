Chapter 01Welcome & Getting Started

Welcome to NEXXUS POS — the modern tablet point-of-sale system built for Caribbean and Americas businesses. This manual covers every screen and feature so you can get the most out of your day-to-day operations.

What you can do with NEXXUS POS

  • Sell faster — touch-friendly cart, barcode scanning, weight-based items, multiple units per product, automatic volume pricing.
  • Manage smarter — real-time stock across multiple locations, ingredient-based recipes for restaurants, vendor purchases, and accounts payable.
  • Grow further — built-in customer loyalty, online store, multi-tenant staff management with role-based permissions.
  • Stay compliant — full double-entry accounting, audit trail of every action, and one-click QuickBooks sync.

What you'll need

  • A modern tablet, laptop, or desktop with a recent browser (Chrome, Edge, Safari, or Firefox).
  • An active NEXXUS POS subscription (a free 14-day trial is available).
  • Optionally: a thermal receipt printer, barcode scanner, cash drawer, and / or weighing scale.
Tip — Install as an app

NEXXUS POS is a Progressive Web App (PWA). On your tablet's browser, open the install prompt to add the app to your home screen. This gives you full-screen mode and offline capability.

Conventions used in this manual

  • Ctrl + S — keyboard shortcuts.
  • Settings → Payment Methods — navigation breadcrumbs (menu path).
  • Admin — feature requires a specific role.
  • New — recently added feature.

Chapter 02Signing In & Account Setup

Whether you're a brand-new business or an existing tenant, here's how to get from zero to your first sale.

Creating a new account

  1. From the marketing site, click Start Free Trial.
  2. Fill in your business name, your name, email, and a strong password (at least 8 characters with a mix of letters and numbers).
  3. Verify your email address by clicking the link we send you. The banner at the top of the dashboard will remind you if verification is still pending.
  4. Choose your business type — Restaurant, Retail, Wholesale, or Hybrid. This pre-enables the right features for you.
  5. Complete the brief onboarding wizard: add your first staff member, set your tax rate, and (optionally) add a location.

Signing in

  1. Visit your tenant URL (typically /app/login).
  2. Enter your email and password and tap Sign In.
  3. If your business has multiple staff members, you'll be prompted for your 4-digit PIN at the start of each shift.
Forgot password?

Click Forgot password? on the sign-in screen. We'll email a reset link that's valid for one hour.

Switching between staff (PIN pad)

At the top-right of the POS, the staff badge shows who is currently logged in for sales. Tap it to bring up the PIN pad and let another team member take over the register without losing the current cart.

Multi-tenant: keeping your data private

Every account is fully isolated. Your products, customers, sales, and reports are only visible to your own staff. NEXXUS POS uses industry-standard authentication tokens (JWT) and encrypts all traffic over HTTPS.

Chapter 04Selling at the Register

The POS is where the magic happens. Designed for one-handed tablet use with large touch targets, it stays out of your way so you can focus on the customer.

Anatomy of the POS screen

  • Product grid (left) — your menu of items, organised by category tabs at the top. Tap to add to cart.
  • Search bar — type a name, SKU, or scan a barcode.
  • Cart (right) — running list of items, with quantity steppers and per-line totals.
  • Order panel — subtotal, tax, discount, tip, and grand total.
  • Action bar — Hold, Discount, Customer, Charge.

Ringing up a sale

  1. Tap items in the product grid (or scan barcodes) to add them to the cart.
  2. Adjust quantities with the + / − buttons, or tap the quantity number to type it directly.
  3. (Optional) Tap Customer to attach a customer for loyalty points.
  4. (Optional) Tap Discount to apply a percentage or fixed-amount discount to the whole sale.
  5. Tap Charge. Choose a payment method — Cash, Card, Split, or any custom method you've configured.
  6. If Cash: enter the amount tendered. Change owed is calculated automatically.
  7. If Split: enter the amount for each method until the total is fully covered.
  8. Tap Complete Sale. The receipt prints (or shows on screen) and the cart resets.

Holding and recalling carts

Need to step away or serve another customer first? Tap Hold to park the current cart with an optional name. Held carts appear in the Held Orders tray — tap to bring any back. Held orders survive page refreshes.

Refunds and voids

  1. Go to Orders in the menu.
  2. Find the original sale (use the date filter or search by receipt number).
  3. Tap Refund to return some or all items. Stock is automatically returned to inventory.
Permission required

Refunds and voids require the Manager or Admin role by default. Configure this under Settings → Roles.

Inventory protection (preventing overselling)

By default, NEXXUS POS will block any sale that would push stock below zero. If a customer tries to buy 5 of an item that only has 3 in stock, you'll see a clear modal: "Only 3 of [item name] available." Adjust the cart and try again.

To allow overselling for specific items (e.g. services, made-to-order, pre-orders), open the product and toggle Allow Overselling.

Chapter 05Cash Drawer & End-of-Shift

Open and close your cash drawer with confidence — every dollar in and out is tracked, including pay-ins, pay-outs, and cash drops.

Opening the register

  1. Tap Cash from the main menu.
  2. Click Open Register.
  3. Enter the starting cash amount (your float / opening balance).
  4. Tap Open Session. The drawer is now active and ready for sales.

During the shift

  • Cash In — record any cash added (e.g. extra change, owner top-up). Choose a reason.
  • Cash Out — record cash taken out (e.g. paying a supplier, petty cash). Always include a reason; this becomes part of your audit trail.
  • Drop — moving cash to the safe. Reduces drawer cash without affecting expected total.

Closing (cash blind count)

  1. Tap Close Register at the end of the shift.
  2. Count the cash drawer physically. Enter the actual amount counted.
  3. NEXXUS calculates the variance (over / short) automatically based on opening cash + cash sales − cash out.
  4. (Optional) Add a closing note explaining any variance.
  5. Tap Close Session. A Z-report is generated and stored under Register.
Z-Reports

Every closed session creates a Z-report — a permanent end-of-shift summary showing sales by payment method, refunds, taxes collected, and variance. Find them under Reports → Z-Reports.

Chapter 06Tables & Restaurant Service

If you run a restaurant or café, the Tables screen turns NEXXUS POS into a full table-service system.

Setting up your floor plan

  1. Go to Tables → Floor Plan.
  2. Add tables one by one (give each a name like "T1", "Patio 3", or "Bar Seat 5").
  3. Set the seat count for each table — useful for reports.
  4. (Optional) Group tables by section (e.g. "Patio", "Main", "Private Room").

Taking orders by table

  1. From the Tables screen, tap an empty table to open a new ticket.
  2. Add items as you would in the POS. Items send to the Kitchen Display when you tap Send to Kitchen.
  3. Tap a busy table again to add more items mid-meal.
  4. When the meal ends, tap Pay to settle the bill (cash, card, or split).

Course management & modifiers

Add modifiers (e.g. "no onions", "extra sauce") and group items into courses (Starters, Mains, Desserts) so the kitchen prepares them in the right order. Set this up in Products → [item] → Modifiers.

Splitting and merging bills

  • Split by item — drag items between guest tabs.
  • Split evenly — divide the total across N guests.
  • Merge — combine two table tickets into one (useful when a party joins another).

Chapter 07Kitchen Display System (KDS)

A second tablet or screen in the kitchen turns NEXXUS POS into a paperless ticket system.

Setup

  1. On your kitchen device, open the same NEXXUS URL and sign in with a kitchen role.
  2. Navigate to Kitchen.
  3. (Optional) Map specific categories to specific stations (Grill, Salads, Bar) under Settings → Kitchen Stations.

Working a ticket

  • New tickets appear at the left, oldest first, with a live timer showing minutes since they came in.
  • Tap an item to mark it In Progress; tap again for Ready.
  • When all items are ready, swipe the ticket off the screen — the table is notified the food is up.
  • Tickets older than your service threshold (default 15 min) turn red so they don't slip through.

Chapter 08Products & Inventory

Your product catalogue is the foundation of every sale, every report, and every stock decision.

Adding a product

  1. Go to Products and tap + New Product.
  2. Fill in the basics: name, category, SKU / barcode, base price, cost.
  3. Set the base unit (each, kg, lb, litre, etc.) — this is how stock is counted internally.
  4. Set the opening stock quantity and a low-stock reorder threshold.
  5. (Optional) Upload a photo. Square images render best on the POS grid.
  6. (Optional) Toggle Sold by weight if this is a per-kg item (see Chapter 10).
  7. Save. The product is immediately live in the POS.

The Edit Product dialog tabs

TabWhat it's for
DetailsName, price, category, photo, SKU, stock thresholds.
VariantsRequired choices like Size or Colour — customers must pick one.
ModifiersOptional add-ons like extra cheese, no onions, side choice.
LocationsPer-store stock when you have multiple outlets (Chapter 11).
Pricing & UnitsVolume tiers and alternate purchase / sale units (Chapter 9).
HistoryStock movement log: every sale, return, transfer, count, and adjustment.

Bulk import / export

Tap the ••• menu on the Products page to Export your full catalogue to CSV, or Import from a CSV template. Use this for bulk price updates, opening stock loads, or migrating from another system.

Stock adjustments & counts

  • Adjust — for one-off corrections (e.g. damaged stock, write-offs). Always requires a reason.
  • Stock Count — full inventory recount; enter actual counted quantity per item, NEXXUS records the variance.
  • Transfer — move stock between locations (Chapter 11).

Chapter 09Volume Pricing & Units of Measure

Sell the same product wholesale, retail, by the case, or by the dozen — without ever creating duplicate items.

Volume pricing tiers

Reward customers who buy more by automatically charging a lower price per unit at higher quantities.

  1. Open a product → Pricing & Units tab.
  2. Under Volume Pricing, tap + Tier.
  3. Enter Min Qty, Max Qty (leave blank for "and up"), and the Unit Price for that band.
  4. Add as many tiers as you like; ranges must not overlap. The open-ended tier (blank max) must be the last one.
  5. Tap Save Pricing. The POS applies the correct tier automatically as customers add quantity to the cart.
Example

Bottled water with base price JMD 200:
Tier 1: 1–11 → JMD 200  ·  Tier 2: 12–47 → JMD 180  ·  Tier 3: 48 and up → JMD 150

Multi-unit sales (Cases, Dozens, Six-packs)

If you sell the same product in multiple packaging — singles, six-packs, cases — set up alternate units instead of duplicating the SKU.

  1. Open a product → Pricing & Units → Purchase / Sale Units section.
  2. Tap + Unit. Enter the unit name (e.g. "Case") and how many base units are in one (e.g. Case = 24).
  3. Toggle Buy for units used when receiving stock; Sell for units shown to cashiers.
  4. Save Units. In the POS, tapping that product opens a Choose Unit popup with a coloured button per sale unit, each showing its computed price.

Stock is always tracked in the base unit, so selling 2 Cases of 24 deducts 48 individual bottles. Volume tiers continue to apply on the total quantity in base units.

Editing or deleting tiers and units

Use the trash icon next to any row to remove it. Always tap Save after edits — closing the dialog without saving discards changes.

Chapter 10Sell by Weight

For deli counters, butchers, produce shops, bulk bins, candy by the pound — anything priced by weight.

Setting up a weight-based product

  1. Create or edit the product. Set the Base Unit to kg, lb, g, or oz.
  2. Toggle Sold by Weight to ON.
  3. Set the price per base unit (e.g. JMD 850 per kg).
  4. Save.

Selling at the register

  1. In the POS, tap the weight item.
  2. The Enter Weight dialog opens. Type the weight from your scale display (e.g. 1.75).
  3. Tap Add to Cart. The line shows "Beef Brisket (1.75 kg) — JMD 1,487.50".
  4. Adjust by tapping the line and entering a new weight if needed.
Connected scales

If you have a USB or serial scale paired in Hardware, the weight field auto-populates from the scale reading. See Chapter 20 for setup.

Chapter 11Multi-Location Stock

Run two storefronts, a warehouse and a shop, or a food truck and a commissary — manage all stock from one account.

Creating locations

  1. Go to Locations and tap + New Location.
  2. Enter the location name, address, and (optionally) a contact phone.
  3. Mark one location as the Primary — sales default here unless overridden.

Per-location stock

On each product's Locations tab you can set a different stock level per outlet. Sales decrement only the location where the sale was rung up. Reports can be filtered per location.

Stock transfers

  1. Go to Locations → Transfers.
  2. Tap + New Transfer.
  3. Choose the source and destination locations.
  4. Add line items with quantities to move.
  5. Mark In Transit when items leave; Received when they arrive (with the actual received quantity, in case of shrinkage).

Chapter 12Customers & Loyalty

Build a regulars list, track purchase history, and reward repeat business automatically.

Adding a customer

  1. Go to Customers and tap + New Customer.
  2. Enter at minimum a name; phone or email helps with lookup later.
  3. (Optional) Add a tax ID for invoicing, a credit limit for AR, and notes (e.g. "prefers stout").

Attaching a customer to a sale

In the POS, tap Customer in the cart action bar. Search by name or phone. Once selected, the customer's name appears at the top of the cart and they earn loyalty points on the sale.

Customer history

Tap any customer to see lifetime spend, average ticket, last visit date, and a complete list of past orders — tap any order for the full receipt detail.

Chapter 13Recipes, Ingredients & Production

For restaurants, bakeries, and manufacturers — track raw ingredients and have NEXXUS automatically deduct them as finished items are sold or produced.

Ingredients (raw materials)

  1. Go to Ingredients and tap + New Ingredient.
  2. Enter the name, base unit (e.g. g, ml, each), cost per unit, and current stock.
  3. Set a low-stock threshold for reorder alerts.

Recipes (linking finished products to ingredients)

  1. Go to Recipes → + New Recipe.
  2. Pick the finished product (e.g. "Beef Burger").
  3. Add ingredient lines — for each, choose the ingredient and the quantity used per one finished unit (e.g. 150 g beef, 1 bun, 2 slices cheese).
  4. NEXXUS computes the recipe cost so you can compare it to the sell price.
  5. Save. From now on, every sale of "Beef Burger" deducts the recipe ingredients automatically.

Production runs

For pre-made batches (e.g. baking 60 loaves overnight, bottling 200 hot sauces), use Production:

  1. Go to Production and tap + New Run.
  2. Choose the finished product and the quantity produced.
  3. Confirm the recipe ingredients to consume.
  4. Mark the run Complete. Ingredients are deducted, finished stock is increased, and a journal entry posts the cost.

Chapter 14Vendors, Purchases & Accounts Payable

Track who you owe, what's coming in, and how much each supplier costs you over time.

Vendors

Under Vendors, add each supplier with their contact info, payment terms (Net 30, Net 60, COD), and tax ID.

Recording a purchase

  1. Go to Purchases → + New Purchase.
  2. Pick the vendor.
  3. Add line items: product, quantity received, unit cost, and (optionally) tax.
  4. Enter invoice number, invoice date, and due date.
  5. Save as Draft to come back to, or Post to commit. Posting:
    • Increases stock by the received quantities
    • Updates the moving-average cost of each item
    • Creates an Accounts Payable entry under the vendor

Raw-material purchases

For ingredient stock (rather than finished products), use Raw Material Purchases — same workflow, but the items are picked from your Ingredients list.

Paying bills

  1. Go to Accounts Payable.
  2. Find the open bill and tap Pay.
  3. Choose the payment method (cash, bank transfer, etc.) and enter the amount.
  4. Partial payments are supported — the bill stays open with a reduced balance.

Chapter 15Accounts Receivable & Credit Sales

Sell on credit, send statements, and track outstanding balances per customer.

Enabling credit for a customer

  1. Open the customer's profile.
  2. Set a Credit Limit (the most they're allowed to owe at any time).
  3. Set Payment Terms (Net 7, Net 30, etc.).

Selling on credit

In the POS, after attaching the customer, choose Credit as the payment method (it appears only for customers with credit enabled). The sale completes and an AR entry is created.

Over-limit warning

If the new sale would exceed the customer's credit limit, NEXXUS warns the cashier. A manager can override at the prompt with their PIN.

Receiving a payment

  1. Go to Accounts Receivable.
  2. Find the customer and tap Receive Payment.
  3. Enter the amount and method. The payment is applied to the oldest open invoices first (FIFO) by default.

Aging report

The AR Aging view shows balances bucketed by days overdue (Current, 1–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90+). Tap any bucket to drill into specific customers.

Chapter 16Reports & Accounting

Operational reports

  • Z-Reports — end-of-shift summaries (Chapter 5).
  • Inventory Valuation — current stock value at cost.
  • Low Stock — items at or below their reorder point.
  • Tax Report — tax collected by rate and period for filing.

Chapter 17Staff, Roles & Permissions

Give every team member the right access — no more, no less.

Adding a staff member

  1. Go to Staff → + New Staff.
  2. Enter their name, email (optional, for sign-in), and a 4-digit PIN.
  3. Assign a role — Cashier, Manager, Admin, or any custom role you create.
  4. (Optional) Restrict them to specific locations.
  5. Save. They can now sign in with their PIN at the POS, and full email/password if assigned.

Default roles

RoleTypical permissions
CashierTake sales, hold/recall carts, attach customers. No refunds, no settings.
ManagerCashier abilities + refunds, voids, discounts, open/close registers, view reports.
AdminEverything: products, settings, staff, billing.
KitchenKitchen Display only.

Custom roles

Under Settings → Roles, create custom roles by ticking exactly which permissions they have (e.g. "Inventory Clerk" with Products + Stock Counts but no sales access).

PIN policy

For security, NEXXUS requires unique 4-digit PINs across staff. PINs are hashed and never stored in plain text.

Chapter 18Settings & Payment Methods

Tailor NEXXUS POS to your business — from your logo on receipts to the exact list of accepted payment methods.

General settings

  • Business profile — name, address, logo, currency, tax rate.
  • Receipt template — header text, footer message ("Thanks for shopping!"), what info to include.
  • Tax — single rate or per-product / per-category overrides.
  • Loyalty — turn on/off, change earn / redeem ratios.
  • Tipping — enable suggested tip % buttons on the payment screen.

Payment Methods

Out of the box, NEXXUS POS includes Cash and Card. Add as many custom methods as you need (e.g. Bank Transfer, Lynk, GoTap, Cheque).

  1. Go to Settings → Payment Methods.
  2. Tap + Add Method. Enter a display name (shown on the payment screen).
  3. Choose a Type — this controls behaviour:
    • Cash — opens cash-tendered input, calculates change.
    • Card — single-tap charge.
    • Other — single-tap, optional reference field.
  4. Tick Enabled to make it appear in the POS.
  5. (Optional) Tick Default — pre-selected on the payment screen.
  6. Drag the row to reorder. Save.
At least one method must be enabled

You can't disable your only enabled method. Add another first, then disable.

Chapter 19Subscription & Billing

Manage your plan, payment method, and renewal — all from inside the app.

Your trial

New accounts start with a 14-day free trial. The dashboard shows days remaining; subscribe before it ends to keep selling without interruption.

Choosing a plan

  1. Go to Subscription.
  2. Compare available plans — Starter, Growth, Pro — by feature and price.
  3. Tap Subscribe on the plan you want.
  4. Enter your card details (PowerTranz / FAC, securely processed) or pay via PayPal.

Cancelling

Chapter 20Hardware, Scales & Receipts

Connect physical devices to make checkout faster and your shop floor more efficient.

Receipt printers

NEXXUS POS supports any browser-printable thermal printer (80 mm, 58 mm). For fully automatic printing without the browser dialog, we recommend an ESC/POS-compatible network printer paired via Hardware → Printers → + Add Printer.

Cash drawers

Most cash drawers are kicked open by the receipt printer (RJ-11 cable). Once your printer is configured, the drawer opens automatically on cash transactions.

Barcode scanners

Plug in any USB / Bluetooth scanner that emulates a keyboard. The POS search bar autofocuses on entry, so scans go straight into product lookup. No setup needed.

Weighing scales

Connect a USB or serial scale and pair it under Hardware → Scales → + Add Scale. Configure baud rate (typically 9600), units (kg/lb), and tare behaviour. The Weight dialog will then auto-fill from the live reading.

Customer-facing display

Open a second browser tab on a side-facing screen and navigate to /store. It mirrors the cart in real time so customers can verify their order as it's being rung up.

Sell mobile phone credit (Digicel, FLOW, others) directly from the POS — powered by Ding Connect.

One-time setup

Top-up needs to be activated by your administrator. Once enabled, a Top-Up button appears on the POS action bar.

Selling top-up

Let customers browse your menu and place orders from any web browser.

Enabling the store

Chapter 23Offline Mode & Syncing

Internet down? You can keep selling. NEXXUS POS queues sales locally and syncs when you're back online.

How it works

  • The PWA caches your product catalogue, prices, and configuration on every successful load.
  • If the network drops, an orange Offline banner appears at the top.
  • You can continue ringing up sales — they're stored in the device's local database.
  • When connectivity returns, the queued sales are submitted in order. The banner shows "Syncing N orders…" until done.
Limits while offline

You cannot create new products, change settings, or process refunds while offline. Stock checks use the last-known quantities; if two devices both sell the same item offline you may end up briefly negative until they sync.

Chapter 24Audit Trail

Every sensitive action is logged — who did it, when, and what changed.

What's tracked

  • Sales, refunds, voids, discounts
  • Product creation, deletion, price changes
  • Stock adjustments, transfers, counts
  • Cash drawer opens, cash in / out, drops, closes
  • Staff sign-ins, role changes, PIN resets
  • Settings changes

Reviewing the trail

Go to Audit. Filter by user, date range, or action type. Tap any row to see before / after values.

Audit log entries cannot be edited or deleted — they're a permanent record for your protection.
